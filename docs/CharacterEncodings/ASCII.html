Quick facts

  • ASCII means American Standard Code for Information Exchange

ASCII is a character encoding standard that is the basis for most character encodings used in the world

ASCII define 128 characters on 7 bits from #00 to #7F.

Control characters are defined from #00 to #1F (32 characters).

Digits are defined from #30 to #39 (10 characters).

Uppercase letters are defined from #41 to #5A (26 characters).

Lowercase letters are defined from #61 to #7A (26 characters).

Remain 34 characters.

Between control characters and digits are 16 characters:

  • ' ': Space
  • '!': Exclamation mark
  • '"': Double quote
  • '#': Number sign
  • '$': Dollar sign
  • '%': Percent sign
  • '&': Ampersand
  • ''': Apostrophe
  • '(': Left parenthesis
  • ')': Right parenthesis
  • '*': Asterisk
  • '+': Plus sign
  • ',': Comma
  • '-': Hyphen and minus sign
  • '.': Period
  • '/': Slash
